# Expense Policy — Frequently Asked Questions

## Q: Are tips reimbursable?
**A:** Yes, tips on meals are reimbursable up to 20% of the pre-tip subtotal. Tips on transportation (taxi, rideshare) are reimbursable up to 15%. Any tip exceeding these percentages will only be reimbursed up to the allowed percentage.

## Q: What happens if I lose a receipt?
**A:** You must complete a Lost Receipt Affidavit (Form EXP-LR-01). The affidavit must include the date, vendor, approximate amount, and business purpose. Both you and your manager must sign it. Note: more than 3 lost receipt affidavits per quarter may trigger an audit.

## Q: Can I expense alcohol?
**A:** Alcohol is generally not reimbursable. The only exception is pre-approved client entertainment events where alcohol is customary (e.g., business dinners). The client entertainment request must be approved by your manager _before_ the event.

## Q: How are international expenses converted?
**A:** Use the exchange rate from the date of the transaction. You can use the rate from xe.com, your credit card statement, or OANDA. Include the source of your exchange rate with your submission.

## Q: What if my hotel exceeds the daily limit?
**A:** Lodging over the $250/night limit requires VP-level pre-approval. If you're traveling to a high-cost city (New York, San Francisco, London, Tokyo), an automatic 30% uplift is applied, making the effective limit $325/night.

## Q: Are personal phone calls reimbursable during travel?
**A:** Personal calls are not reimbursable. However, if your personal phone plan incurred international roaming charges solely because of business travel, you may expense up to $15/day for connectivity.

## Q: What is the mileage reimbursement rate?
**A:** The current mileage rate is $0.67/mile (2024 IRS standard rate). You must log the starting location, destination, total miles, and business purpose for each trip.

## Q: How quickly will I be reimbursed?
**A:** Once your expense report is approved, reimbursement is processed within 10 business days and deposited into your payroll account.

## Q: Do I need separate expense reports for different trips?
**A:** Yes. Each business trip should have its own expense report for audit and budget tracking purposes.
